FPL EPL

Fantasy football is a game in which participants assemble an imaginary team of real life football players and score points based on those players actual statistical performance or their perceived contribution on the field of play.

A football statistical index, it has been developed specifically to give a verdict on a player as an FPL asset. It uses match-event data to generate a single score for three key areas: Influence, Creativity and Threat. These figures then combine to create an individualâ€™s ICT Index score.

This is where the FPL check comes into play. check fpl will analyze the ICT data and display it in the form of a radar graph. When you are confused about which player to transfer, check fpl will help you compare data between the two players. let's check this out.

Getting Started

First from home page you need to scroll down and you will find two drop down form. to compare 2 players you have to choose two teams and 2 players from the team to compare.



Under the Form, you will find 2 different buttons, namely the green button which will display data, where the data is higher if the value is better and the yellow button which will display smaller data is better. For examples of data displayed from higher is better are the number of goals, the number of assists, the index of creativity data, the number of saves (optimal for goalkeepers), and the number of clean sheets (optimal for defenders and goalkeepers). While the data from lower is better is the number of goals conceded, the price, the value of the price reduction, yellow cards, goals per minute, and assists per minute.



From the data above, which is two graphs of data from Sadio Mane and Phil Foden are shown. From there we can determine which player is better. It can be seen from the "higher is better" data in the green box on the left. Mane clearly has more goals, total points, and creates more chances than Phil Foden, but Phil Foden makes more assists than Sadio Mane. Obviously, from this data, Mane is superior, but let's look at the chart on the right with yellow box color, namely the "lower is better" graph. There we see how effective it is if we have Phil Foden. With a much lower price and a very stable price (price_fall) from Mane, Phil Foden has better statistics, namely the number of goals and assists per minute is better than Mane, and the number of goals conceded is less.
